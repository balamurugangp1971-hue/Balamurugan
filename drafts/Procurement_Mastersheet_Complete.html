Universal Price Anatomy — What Every Quote Contains
Supplier Price = Raw Materials + Direct Labour + Manufacturing Overhead + Logistics & Packaging + SG&A + Profit Margin + Risk Premium
Raw Materials
40–60% of total cost. Link to LME / commodity indices. Most negotiable element.
Direct Labour
20–35% in labour-intensive industries. Varies by country, skill, and automation level.
Overhead + SG&A
15–25% typical. Factory overhead + admin + sales. Often padded. Benchmark against industry.
Profit Margin
5–20% fair range. Below 5% = supplier cuts quality. Above 20% = negotiation opportunity.

The PRICE Framework — 5-Step Bottom Price Discovery
  • P
    Production Cost Deconstruction — Break every quote into raw material, labour, overhead, logistics, and margin. Use Should-Cost Analysis. Never negotiate a black-box price.
  • R
    Route Intelligence — Map every intermediary between factory and your dock. Each layer adds 10–25%. Direct manufacturer sourcing saves 15–25% immediately.
  • I
    Index-Based Benchmarking — Link raw material cost to live indices: LME (metals), Platts (energy), CRISIL (domestic). If copper is down 12%, your cable quote should reflect it.
  • C
    Competitive Leverage — Minimum 3 quotes every RFQ. Multi-round negotiation. Volume consolidation. Long-term commitment in exchange for below-market pricing.
  • E
    Execution & Lock-in — Formalise with price escalation/de-escalation clauses indexed to commodities. Annual should-cost review built into contract. Prevents silent inflation.

Inventory Management Formulas — Field-Verified
EOQ — Economic Order Quantity
EOQ = √(2 × D × S / H)

D = Annual Demand (units)
S = Ordering Cost per order (₹)
H = Holding Cost per unit per year (₹)

Use: Minimise total inventory cost
Safety Stock
SS = Z × σ(lead time demand)

Z = 1.65 for 95% service level
Z = 1.96 for 97.5% service level
Z = 2.33 for 99% service level

Use: Buffer against demand variability
Reorder Point (ROP)
ROP = (Avg Daily Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock

Use: Trigger purchase order automatically
ABC–XYZ Classification Matrix
ABC (Value):
A = 80% value, 20% SKUs → daily review
B = 15% value → weekly review
C = 5% value → monthly/min-max auto

XYZ (Demand Pattern):
X = Predictable · Y = Variable · Z = Erratic
AX = Most critical · CZ = Consider elimination
