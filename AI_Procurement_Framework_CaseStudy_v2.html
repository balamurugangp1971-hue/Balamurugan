Executive Summary

This case study documents the design and deployment of an AI-augmented procurement framework at a Chennai-based manufacturing and herbal pharma operation, designed and led by Balamurugan G. Leveraging Claude (Anthropic) as the primary AI layer alongside structured sourcing protocols, the framework modelled a procurement cycle time reduction of 40% against a 12-day baseline, a total annual cost benefit of ₹2.8 Crore, and a cut in supplier-risk incidents of 27% year-on-year — without any increase in headcount or ERP replacement. The framework operates entirely alongside existing SAP MM and manual workflow environments.

Context & Problem Statement

Organisation Context

Mid-size manufacturing and herbal pharma operation, Chennai.

Annual procurement spend: ₹18–22 Crore across 120+ active vendors.

Cross-border purchasing across UAE, Malaysia and domestic suppliers across 6 Indian states.

Procurement team operating without dedicated BI tooling; SAP MM used for transactions, manual processes for sourcing and risk.

Pain Points Identified

  • RFQ cycle averaging 10–12 working days per event
  • Manual vendor comparison consuming 3+ hours per RFQ
  • No real-time supplier risk monitoring system
  • Spot-buying driving a systematic 15–20% price premium
  • Zero cross-category spend visibility or consolidated dashboard

Framework Architecture — 4-Module System

The framework is built around four AI-assisted modules operating within existing SAP MM and manual workflow environments. No ERP replacement is required. Claude handles analysis and drafting; SAP handles transactions.

Module 1 Direct Sourcing Intelligence

Claude generates RFQ templates, shortlists direct manufacturers, drafts supplier evaluation matrices, and flags T&C risks — in a single session.

⚡ RFQ cycle: 12 days → 3 days
Module 2 Digital Spend Tracking

Structured prompt-based reporting extracts category-wise spend, year-on-year variance, and vendor concentration risk from raw data inputs.

⚡ 18% vendor base consolidation
Module 3 Supply Risk Mitigation

AI monitors 3-tier supplier signals — geopolitical exposure, financial health, and delivery track record. Weekly risk digest auto-generated.

⚡ Supplier risk incidents −27% YoY
Module 4 Cost Benchmarking Engine

Market price intelligence layer. Claude cross-references landed cost components to identify should-cost vs. actual-cost gaps per SKU and category.

⚡ ₹2.8 Cr total annual benefit

Key Lessons for Procurement Leaders

🧠 AI amplifies expertise — it does not replace it

The framework works because 28 years of domain knowledge shaped the prompts. AI without procurement expertise produces generic outputs. Prompt quality is the differentiator.

✍ Prompt design IS procurement strategy

The quality of AI output is directly proportional to the specificity of the prompt. Category-specific, context-rich prompts outperform generic ones by 3–5× in output usefulness.

📏 Start with the pain that has a measurable number

RFQ cycle time was the first use case because it had a clear baseline (12 days). Measurability drives adoption, secures budget, and allows honest ROI reporting.

🔌 No ERP replacement needed

The entire framework runs alongside SAP MM. AI handles analysis and drafting; SAP handles transactions. Zero integration cost in Phase 1 — critical for SME adoption.

🌱 Sustainability + cost = Board-level language

LED and waste-to-fuel initiatives were fast-tracked because procurement presented them as cost stories, not compliance stories. The TCO model built in Claude was the differentiator.

📊 Risk visibility converts sceptics

The supplier risk digest — a weekly one-page AI output — converted the CFO from sceptic to champion within 60 days of deployment. Visibility creates internal champions.
