📊 Universal Price Anatomy

How Any Product Price is Constructed

Every product's landed cost is composed of five layers. Understanding these allows procurement professionals to identify true cost drivers and negotiate leverage points.

Layer Definition Typical % Range Negotiation Lever
1. Raw Material Cost Commodity cost of inputs (steel, plastic, chemicals, etc.) 25–45% Volume lock-in contracts; commodity hedging
2. Manufacturing Overhead Factory labor, utilities, equipment depreciation 15–30% Capacity utilization; process automation partnerships
3. Intermediary Markup Distributor, agent, or reseller margin 10–40% Direct sourcing; bypassing middlemen (30–75% savings possible)
4. Logistics & Insurance Freight, warehousing, customs, insurance 5–15% Negotiated incoterms; freight consolidation; duty optimization
5. Margin & Profit Supplier's operating margin + profit 8–20% Volume commitments; long-term partnerships
🎯 Channel Bypass Strategies (30–75% Savings)

Direct Manufacturer Sourcing

  • Eliminate: Distributor margin (10–40%)
  • Direct to: Factory, sub-contract manufacturer, or OEM
  • Typical savings: 15–25% on landed cost
  • Trade-off: MOQ typically higher; payment terms stricter

Cross-Border Direct Import

  • Eliminate: Domestic distributor (20–35%)
  • Direct from: UAE, Malaysia, China, Turkey, Vietnam
  • Typical savings: 20–35% vs. domestic
  • Trade-off: Customs, documentation, extended lead time

Consolidation & Cluster Sourcing

  • Combine: 5–10 SKUs in one RFQ to single vendor
  • Negotiate: Volume discount across categories
  • Typical savings: 8–15% on blended basket
  • Trade-off: Single-source risk; need robust backup plan

Secondary Market / Refurbished

  • Source: Refurbished, cosmetic-grade, or off-spec inventory
  • Vendor base: Liquidators, overstock brokers, OEM secondary channels
  • Typical savings: 30–60% on specific categories
  • Trade-off: Quality variance; availability unpredictable

💰 PRICE Framework — Universal Cost Deconstruction

P – Production Cost Deconstruction

Break down unit cost: raw material + labor + overhead. Identify if supplier is making excessive margin or if commodity input cost has dropped. Cross-reference with published commodity indices (LME, NCDEX, etc.).

R – Route Intelligence

Map supply chain route: manufacturer → distributor → agent → end-user. Identify intermediaries adding markup. Direct sourcing eliminates 1–3 layers. Cross-border import optimizes incoterms (CIF vs. FOB).

I – Intermediary Margin Mapping

Calculate each intermediary's margin. Distributors typically take 15–40%. Direct manufacturer pricing reveals true margin. Negotiate margin reduction or bypass entirely. Create tiered pricing structure: volume ≥ 100 units = 20% discount.

C – Competitive Leverage

Benchmark against 3–5 competing suppliers. Create RFQ with transparent specifications. Run mini-auction (3 rounds) or reverse bidding. Leverage data to negotiate: "Competitor Y quoted ₹X; can you match?"

E – Execution & Lock-In

Once negotiated, lock in terms for 12–24 months via contract. Include escalation clause tied to commodity index (e.g., ±5% if raw material cost moves >10%). Document SLA, quality, lead time, warranty, and exit clause.
