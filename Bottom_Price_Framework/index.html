PROPRIETARY FRAMEWORK

📉 Bottom Price Framework

A structured methodology to identify the true market floor price for any commodity or service — eliminating over-payment and strengthening negotiation position.

What is the Bottom Price?

The Bottom Price is the lowest sustainable price a supplier can offer while still covering their costs and a minimum acceptable margin. It is NOT the price you pay — it is the anchor you negotiate from. Knowing the bottom price gives buyers the power to negotiate with data, not guesswork.

Bottom Price = Raw Material Cost + Conversion Cost + Overheads + Minimum Margin (3–5%)
5-Step Framework
1

Cost Decomposition

Break the product into raw material, labour, energy, packaging, and logistics cost components. Use BOM (Bill of Materials) analysis to map each element with market-referenced rates.

2

Market Benchmarking

Reference commodity indices (MCX, LME, CRISIL) for raw material rates. Validate against 3 alternate supplier quotes. Identify price variance and outliers.

3

Supplier Margin Analysis

Use industry standard margin ranges (3–8% for manufacturing, 10–15% for trading) to compute the minimum price a supplier can offer without making a loss.

4

Should-Cost Modelling

Build a Should-Cost model that represents what the item ought to cost if produced efficiently. This becomes the benchmark for supplier negotiation.

5

Negotiation Anchoring

Use the bottom price as the walk-away floor. Open negotiations 15–20% above bottom price to create room for concessions while protecting value.
