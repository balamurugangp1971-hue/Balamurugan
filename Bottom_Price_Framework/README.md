# Bottom Price Framework (BPF)

A strategic procurement framework designed to identify, negotiate, and secure the absolute baseline market price for direct and indirect materials without compromising quality or supply security.

## 📈 Key Impact Metrics
* **Cost Savings Achieved:** ₹1.5 Crore+ in cost avoidance and direct savings.
* **Spend Managed:** Applied across portfolios exceeding ₹50 Crore+.
* **Supplier Rationalization:** Improved strategic vendor alignment by 20%.

---

## 🛠️ The 4-Pillar Framework Methodology

### 1. Clean-Sheet Cost Modeling (Should-Cost Analysis)
* Deconstructing the supplier's cost structures (Raw materials, conversion costs, overheads, and logical margins).
* Utilizing index-based pricing linked to global market standards (e.g., LME, Platts, local commodity exchanges).

### 2. Market Intelligence & Benchmarking
* Cross-industry data gathering to map supplier capacities and utilization rates.
* Evaluating macro-economic factors influencing raw material price cycles.

### 3. Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Evaluation
* Moving beyond unit price to evaluate logistics, payment terms, inventory carrying costs, and quality compliance.
* Quantifying hidden risks in the tier-2 and tier-3 supply chain.

### 4. Leveraged Negotiation Strategy
* Consolidating volumes to maximize purchasing power.
* Deploying multi-round, fact-based negotiations backed by the "should-cost" data models.
